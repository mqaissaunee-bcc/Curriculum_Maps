Curriculum Mapping

Program Name: Electronics Tech Program, Elect/Computer Tech Option, A.A.S
Program Code: ELCMP
Note: ENGI103 (Engineering Problem Solving with Computers) has been substituted for COMP135 (Computer Architecture Using Assembly Language) in this curriculum mapping. This substitution reflects current program requirements.

Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs)

PLO 1: Build, test, operate and maintain electrical systems
PLO 2: Apply circuit analysis, problem solving and design, computer programming, analog and digital electronics, and microcomputers to the building, testing, operation, and maintenance of electrical/electronic(s) systems
PLO 3: Apply scientific concepts to electrical/electronic(s) circuits in a rigorous mathematical environment at or above the level of algebra and trigonometry
PLO 4: Work effectively in diverse teams and provide leadership to teams and organizations
PLO 5: Communicate effectively in oral, graphic, and written form

Key Findings

Strong Coverage Areas

  • PLO 1 (Build & Test Systems): Well-covered across 8 of 11 courses, with particularly strong hands-on components in ELEC103, ELEC111, ELEC112, and ELEC225
  • PLO 2 (Circuit Analysis & Design): Comprehensive coverage in all electronics courses (ELEC series), with computational support from ENGI103 and NETW courses
  • PLO 3 (Mathematical Foundations): Strong support through dedicated math courses (MATH152, MATH153) and rigorous application in all ELEC courses
  • PLO 5 (Communication): Consistently addressed through laboratory reports and technical documentation requirements across multiple courses

Areas Requiring Attention

  • PLO 4 (Teamwork & Leadership): Critical gap identified - only ELEC225 explicitly addresses teamwork. Recommendation: Add collaborative projects or team-based activities to additional courses, particularly ELEC241, ENGI103, and senior-level courses
  • ENGI103 Team Integration: Consider adding group programming projects to strengthen PLO 4 coverage

Course Substitution Impact

  • ENGI103 vs. COMP135: ENGI103 (MATLAB programming) provides stronger support for PLO 2 (problem-solving and programming) and PLO 3 (mathematical applications) compared to the original assembly language course
  • The substitution enhances the program's focus on computational problem-solving tools relevant to modern engineering practice

Program Strengths

  • Excellent progression from foundational skills (ELEC103) through circuit theory (ELEC111, ELEC112) to advanced applications (ELEC225, ELEC241)
  • Strong integration of theoretical knowledge and practical application through laboratory work
  • Comprehensive mathematical preparation supporting technical coursework
  • Balanced coverage of analog and digital electronics

Recommendations

  • Enhance PLO 4 coverage by incorporating team-based projects in at least 2-3 additional courses
  • Consider adding peer review activities to strengthen both teamwork and communication skills
  • Document any informal teamwork activities already occurring in courses where mapping shows no PLO 4 coverage
