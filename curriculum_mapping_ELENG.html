Curriculum Mapping

Program Name: Electronics Technology, Electrical Engineering Technology Option, A.A.S
Program Code: ELENG
Course Substitutions: This curriculum mapping reflects the following program modifications from the original Electronics Program:
  • ENGI103 (Engineering Problem Solving with Computers) replaces COMP135 and COMP137, providing stronger computational problem-solving skills relevant to modern engineering practice
  • PHYS111 (General Physics I) and PHYS112 (General Physics II) are included as essential physics foundations for electrical engineering
  • MATH171 (Calculus I) replaces MATH152 and MATH153, providing calculus-based mathematical foundations required for engineering analysis
  • ECON107 (Economics) is included to provide understanding of economic principles in engineering decisions

Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs)

PLO 1: Perform engineering analysis and problem solving
PLO 2: Develop an engineering design to meet given specifications
PLO 3: Work effectively in diverse teams and provide leadership to teams and organizations
PLO 4: Communicate effectively in oral, graphic, and written form

Key Findings

Strong Coverage Areas

  • PLO 1 (Analysis & Problem Solving): Exceptionally comprehensive coverage across all courses, with particularly strong emphasis in ELEC series, ENGI103, MATH171, and Physics courses. The calculus-based mathematics and physics provide rigorous analytical foundations
  • PLO 2 (Engineering Design): Well-covered in electronics courses (ELEC series) with hands-on design components, computational design tools (ENGI103, ELEC101), and practical applications throughout the curriculum
  • PLO 4 (Communication): Consistently addressed through laboratory reports, technical documentation, and professional communication requirements across multiple courses

Areas Requiring Attention

  • PLO 3 (Teamwork & Leadership): Critical gap identified - only ELEC225 explicitly addresses teamwork. This is a significant deficiency for an engineering technology program. Recommendation: Add collaborative projects or team-based activities to at least 3-4 additional courses, particularly ELEC241, ENGI103, and senior-level courses
  • ENGI103 Integration: Consider adding group programming projects to strengthen PLO 3 coverage while reinforcing computational skills
  • ECON107 Application: Economic principles should be explicitly connected to engineering decision-making scenarios to strengthen PLO 1 mapping

Program Substitution Impact

  • ENGI103 vs. COMP135/COMP137: ENGI103 (MATLAB programming) provides superior support for engineering problem-solving (PLO 1) and computational design (PLO 2) compared to assembly language and general programming courses. Modern engineering computational tools are better aligned with industry needs
  • MATH171 vs. MATH152/MATH153: Calculus I provides essential mathematical foundations for engineering analysis, particularly for understanding rates of change, optimization, and advanced circuit analysis required in electrical engineering
  • PHYS111/PHYS112 Addition: Physics courses provide critical foundations for understanding electrical phenomena, electromagnetism, and circuit behavior at a deeper theoretical level required for engineering technology
  • ECON107 Addition: Economics provides context for engineering decision-making, cost analysis, and understanding market forces that impact engineering projects

Program Strengths

  • Excellent mathematical preparation: Calculus I provides rigorous analytical tools for electrical engineering
  • Strong integration of theory and practice through laboratory work in ELEC series
  • Comprehensive progression from foundational skills through advanced applications
  • Modern computational tools (MATLAB, PSpice) integrated throughout curriculum
  • Physics foundations provide deep understanding of electrical phenomena
  • Balanced coverage of analog and digital electronics

Recommendations

  • Priority: Enhance PLO 3 coverage by incorporating team-based projects in at least 3-4 courses beyond ELEC225
  • Add peer review activities to strengthen both teamwork and communication skills
  • Explicitly connect ECON107 content to engineering decision-making and project economics
  • Consider adding a capstone project that integrates teamwork, design, analysis, and communication
  • Document any informal teamwork activities already occurring in courses where mapping shows no PLO 3 coverage

Additional Notes

This curriculum mapping reflects the ELENG program structure with significant modifications from the standard Electronics Technology program, including:
  • ENGI103 replaces COMP135 and COMP137 for stronger computational foundations
  • MATH171 (Calculus I) replaces MATH152 and MATH153 for rigorous mathematical preparation
  • PHYS111 and PHYS112 added for essential physics foundations
  • ECON107 added for economic analysis and decision-making skills
The mapping is based on official course syllabi and program learning outcomes as documented in program materials.
